# Goal standard

## Placement rules

Do not create goals simply to fill a form.

Use an individual goal when a person has a career-growth ambition or wants to deliberately develop skills beyond their current level. The goal must describe a meaningful change the person will own, not simply work they will perform.

Distinguish goals from adjacent work:

- Normal feature and project delivery belongs in the roadmap.
- Behaviour already expected at someone's level belongs in role expectations and feedback.
- Courses, reading, conferences, and exploration belong in the development plan or as milestones supporting a goal.
- Career-growth goals connect the individual's ambition to observable evidence that they are ready for broader responsibility.

If a goal can be completed without producing a meaningful improvement, rewrite it or move it to the roadmap, role expectations, or development plan.

## Goal structure

### Goal title

State the outcome or improvement, not the activity.

### Goal description

Explain the current problem, why it matters, the scope, and what the individual owns.

### Success criteria

Define the evidence, target, and deadline that will show whether the goal succeeded.

## Review checks

- Is the goal connected to an individual career-growth ambition or a specific skill they want to develop?
- Does the title state an outcome rather than an activity?
- Does the description explain the problem, importance, scope, and ownership?
- Do the success criteria include evidence, a target, and a deadline?
- Is the result beyond normal role or delivery expectations?
- Can progress be reviewed during the year?
- For a shared goal, is each person's individual contribution clear?

## Examples

### Database incident diagnosis

Weak title: Complete Datadog fundamentals training

Better title: Reduce database incident diagnosis time

Training may be a milestone, but completing it is not the outcome.

Goal description:

Database incidents currently take too long to diagnose because the investigation approach is inconsistent. The individual will establish and introduce a repeatable Datadog investigation workflow for the database team.

Success criteria:

- Workflow documented and adopted by the team by the end of Q3.
- Applied in at least three real incidents or simulations.
- Diagnosis time reduced from `[baseline]` to `[target]`.
- Feedback collected and the workflow updated based on actual use.

### Constructive feedback

Weak title: Give constructive feedback throughout 2026

Better title: Reduce recurring team delivery blockers

Possible success criteria:

- Identify the three most significant blockers.
- Agree corrective actions.
- Implement at least 80 percent of them by Q4.
- Demonstrate improvement through reduced carryover, blocked work, or lead time.

Giving feedback may be a method, but the goal is the improvement it helps produce.

### Growth toward Senior Engineer

Weak title: Grow toward Senior Engineer

Better title: Independently lead a backend initiative from definition through production

Possible success criteria:

- Deliver one bounded initiative by Q4.
- Own routine technical decisions without unnecessary escalation.
- Coordinate dependencies and stakeholders.
- Complete a post-release evaluation of the result.

The stronger version turns a broad ambition into observable evidence of readiness for broader responsibility.
